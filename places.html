First Place

Lake District National Park

Why is the Lake District national park famous? Image result for lake district national park A popular holiday destination, it is famous for its lakes, forests, and mountains, and its associations with William Wordsworth and other Lake Poets and also with Beatrix Potter and John Ruskin. The Lake District National Park was established in 1951 and covers an area of 2,362 square kilometres (912 sq mi)

Third Place

Westfield Fishery - Carp Fishing Lake

Westfield Fishery is situated in the picturesque village of Medmenham which is located half way between Marlow in Buckinghamshire and Henley on Thames in Oxfordshire. The carp fishing lake was formed following gravel extraction and is located on a private estate, offering a peaceful and tranquil setting that can be enjoyed all year round. Here at Westfield Fishery we have both a 3 acre carp fishing lake and ½ mile long coarse stream, both offering a range of different types of fish to catch. Both the lake and the stream are well stocked, with the carp lake offering a great selection of common carp and mirror carp (6 over 30lb’s and in excess of 38 lbs) plus crucian carp and a selection of large tench, bream and pike.

Fourth Place

The River Dove and the Peak District

A legendary fishing spot since Izaak Walton’s The Compleat Angler was published in 1653; the Dove runs for 40 miles through spectacular Peak District hills and dales. It rises on Axe Edge near Buxton and meets the mighty Trent just north of Burton. The river is popular with fly anglers, offering high-quality trout and grayling. Coarse anglers will also enjoy plentiful chub and barbel as well as roach, bream, and pike.

Fifth Place

Windermere and the Lake District

Windermere, England’s biggest lake, is home to pike, perch, eels and other coarse favourites. The game fishing is just as appealing, with rare arctic charr and brown trout in abundance. Best of all, salmon and sea trout migrate through the lake in the spring. Other nearby lakes are just as tempting, with twenty-pound pike frequently taken from Grasmere and Rydal Water.
